# Key Problems

The National Library of Australia is seeking a phishing simulation solution to strengthen its cyber security awareness program.

While a phishing simulation solution is currently in operation, several capability gaps limit its effectiveness in preparing staff for modern phishing threats.

---

## 1. Lack of Realistic Phishing Simulations

The current solution relies on generic or predictable phishing emails that do not adequately reflect sophisticated modern attack techniques.

### Requirements

- Highly targeted phishing campaigns
- Context-aware phishing scenarios
- Spear phishing simulations
- Business Email Compromise (BEC) simulations
- Credential harvesting simulations
- Real-world attack emulation

---

## 2. Limited Flexibility in Campaign Design

Existing tools provide limited flexibility when creating and managing campaigns.

### Requirements

- Comprehensive phishing template library
- Easily customised templates aligned to organisational context
- Multiple phishing payload variations within a single campaign
- Different phishing variants distributed across user groups or individuals
- Support for varying attack difficulty levels
- Support for multiple attack types
- Ability to target specific users or organisational branches

---

## 3. Predictable Campaign Execution

Current campaigns deliver similar payloads simultaneously, reducing realism and potentially influencing user behaviour.

### Requirements

- Randomised phishing delivery
- Staggered campaign execution
- Delivery across varying time periods
- Always-on phishing simulations rather than point-in-time testing

---

## 4. Limited Visibility into User Risk

Current reporting provides insufficient insight into user behaviour.

### Requirements

- Individual-level reporting
- Team-level reporting
- Enterprise-level reporting
- Tracking of:
  - Email clicks
  - Credential submissions
  - Email replies
  - Phishing reports
  - Other user actions
- Identification of high-risk users
- Behavioural trend analysis

---

## 5. Managing Repeat Offenders

Repeat offenders represent increased organisational risk but are not currently managed effectively.

### Requirements

- Identification of repeat offenders
- Flagging repeat offenders
- Targeted advanced simulations
- Increased simulation frequency for high-risk users
- User risk scoring
- Prioritised remediation

---

## 6. Continuous Improvement

The current solution lacks a strong measurement and feedback capability.

### Requirements

- Phish-prone percentage
- Reporting rates
- Resilience scores
- Benchmarking
- Historical trend analysis
- Continuous improvement metrics
