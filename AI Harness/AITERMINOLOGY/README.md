1. Artificial Intelligence (AI): The simulation of human intelligence processes by
machines, especially computer systems. This includes learning, reasoning, and self-
correction.
2. Machine Learning (ML): A subset of AI involving the study of computer algorithms that
improve automatically through experience and by the use of data.
3. Neural Networks: A series of algorithms that attempt to recognize underlying
relationships in a set of data through a process that mimics the way the human brain
operates.
4. Deep Learning: A subset of ML based on artificial neural networks with representation
learning. It involves learning from data that is unstructured or unlabeled.
5. Natural Language Processing (NLP): A field of AI that focuses on the interaction
between computers and humans through natural language.
6. Predictive AI: Predicted AI focuses on Specialized AI models, which provide future
outcomes based on historical data. These are targeted use cases like fraud detection,
credit rating etc. This form of AI has been around for a long time.
7. Generative AI: Generative AI is a branch of AI focused on generative new content- text,
videos, music, code and images. LLMs are tailored for text-based tasks such as natural
language understanding, text generation, language translation, and textual analysis.
8. Large Language Models: Large Language Models (LLMs) are a subset of artificial
intelligence (AI) that are designed to understand and generate human-like text based on
vast amounts of data. They use deep learning techniques, particularly a type of neural
network known as a Transformer, to process and generate language. The deep
understanding, makes LLMs useful in responding to general prompts at light speed.
LLMs are neural networks, typically measured by a number of parameters. Examples of
the number of parameters in a model are 7 billion, 13 billion, 70 billion, and higher.
9. Transformer- A transformer is a type of neural network architecture in artificial
intelligence (AI) that can change an input sequence into an output sequence.
Transformers do this by learning context and relationships between components of a
sequence. For example, if the input sequence is &quot;What is the color of the sky?&quot;, a
transformer model can use an internal mathematical representation to identify the
relationship between the words &quot;color&quot;, &quot;sky&quot;, and &quot;blue&quot;.
10. GPT- A Generative Pre-trained Transformer (GPT) is a type of large language model
developed by OpenAI. It&#39;s designed to understand and generate human-like text based
on large-scale training data
11. Computer Vision: An interdisciplinary field that deals with how computers can gain
high-level understanding from digital images or videos.
12. Training: In the context of artificial intelligence (AI), training refers to the process of
teaching an AI model to perform specific tasks by exposing it to a large amount of data
and allowing it to learn patterns, make decisions, and improve its performance over time.
This is a critical phase in the development of machine learning models, including neural
networks, decision trees, support vector machines, and others.
13. Inferencing: Inferencing in artificial intelligence refers to the process of drawing logical
conclusions from available information. It involves using rules and logical reasoning to
make logical deductions and reach a solution or decision. Inferencing is a key
component of many AI systems, especially in tasks such as problem-solving, decision-
making, and pattern recognition. It allows AI systems to make hypotheses, predictions,
and recommendations based on the data and rules it has been trained on. Inferencing is
an important aspect of creating intelligent systems that can mimic human thinking and
decision-making processes.
14. Fine-Tuning: Fine-tuning in the context of AI refers to the process of taking a pre-trained
model and making small adjustments to its parameters using a smaller, task-specific
dataset. This approach leverages the knowledge already embedded in a pre-trained
model, which has typically been trained on a large, general dataset, to adapt it for a
more specialized or specific task. Fine-tuning is widely used in deep learning, especially
with models like BERT, GPT, and other large neural networks.
15. Customizing: Customizing in the context of AI refers to tailoring an AI model or system
to meet specific requirements or preferences of a particular application, user, or
organization. This process can involve adjusting various aspects of the AI model,
including its architecture, parameters, training data, and deployment environment, to
ensure it performs optimally for the intended use case.
16. Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) is a technique for enhancing the accuracy
and reliability of generative AI models with facts fetched from external sources through a
database. It fills a gap in how LLMs work.
17. Ethics in AI: The branch of ethics that evaluates the moral implications and
responsibilities of AI technology and applications.
18. AI Bias: Systematic and repeatable errors in AI systems that create unfair outcomes,
such as privileging one arbitrary group over another.
19. Reinforcement Learning (RL): An area of ML where an agent learns to behave in an
environment by performing actions and seeing the results.
20. Supervised Learning: A type of ML where the model is trained on labeled data, which
means the input comes with the correct output.
21. Unsupervised Learning: A type of ML where the model is trained on unlabeled data
and must find structure in its input.
